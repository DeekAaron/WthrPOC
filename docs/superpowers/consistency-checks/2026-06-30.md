## Vocabulary drift

### V1: Term "tab" / "day-tab"
**Defined in `business-domain-context.md` as:** the **Forecast Day** entry — _"One day within the **Forecast** — the unit the user selects to view."_ The definition explicitly lists **_Avoid_: day card, tab, date.**
**Used in `PRD.md` as:** _"I want the Forecast presented as one tab per Forecast Day"_ (req 7), _"today's tab to feature the live Current Conditions"_ (req 8), _"every other day's tab"_ (req 9); and **`Roadmap.md` F3** as _"day-tabs with Daily Summaries"_.
**Suggested resolution:** Decide which way to bend. Either (a) accept "tab" as the UI affordance that renders a Forecast Day and add a one-line note to the glossary entry ("rendered as a tab in the UI") so the avoid-list no longer contradicts the product docs, or (b) rephrase PRD/Roadmap in terms of "Forecast Day" and reserve "tab" for incidental UI description. (a) is the lighter touch and matches how the term is actually used.

### V2: Term "result" (as a synonym for Candidate)
**Defined in `business-domain-context.md` as:** the **Candidate** — _"One resolved place returned by geocoding a **Search Query**."_ The definition explicitly lists **_Avoid_: result, match, suggestion, hit.**
**Used in `PRD.md` as:** _"picking a Saved Location to behave exactly like a fresh search result"_ (req 15); echoed in **`Roadmap.md`** F2 and F5 as _"behaves exactly like a fresh search result."_
**Suggested resolution:** Minor — "search result" reads as the outcome of searching rather than a hard synonym, but it brushes the avoid-list. Prefer "behaves exactly like picking a fresh **Candidate**" in the PRD/Roadmap, or add "(informally, a search result)" to the Candidate glossary entry if the phrase is worth keeping.
